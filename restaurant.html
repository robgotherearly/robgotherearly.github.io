Zomato Restaurant Data Analysis

Zomato Project Overview

Overview

Zomato operates in over 15,000 cities and serves a user base of approximately 7,700 individuals. The platform has achieved a total revenue of $989 million through 150,000 orders, delivering 2 million quantities of food items. The food categories are divided into three main types:

  • Foods without vegetables: $106 million
  • Foods with vegetables: $122 million
  • Other foods: $24 million

Annual Sales Growth

  • 2017: $9 million
  • 2018: $41 million
  • 2019: $34 million
  • 2020: $14 million

Top Performing Cities

  • Tirupati: $42 million
  • Electronic City: $28 million
  • Baner: $26 million

Bikaner stands out for having the highest ratings and the most active users.

User Performance

Active Users: 78,000

Total Ratings: 148,000

User Age Distribution:

  • 23 years old: 14,500 users
  • 22 years old: 11,400 users
  • 24 years old: 10,500 users

User Acquisition and Retention:

  • Total Gained Users: 12,000 (6,500 male, 5,100 female)
  • Total Lost Users: 33,000 (19,000 male, 14,000 female)

Analysis

Revenue Distribution and Food Preferences:

Foods with vegetables have the highest sales, followed by foods without vegetables and other foods. This indicates a preference for healthier meal options among users.

Sales Growth Analysis:

The sales figures show a significant peak in 2018, followed by a decline in subsequent years. The growth in 2018 suggests a period of rapid expansion or successful marketing campaigns, while the decline in later years could indicate market saturation, increased competition, or changing consumer preferences.

Geographic Performance:

Tirupati, Electronic City, and Baner are the top-performing cities. Bikaner's top ratings and active users highlight it as a strategic location for further investments and targeted marketing.

User Demographics:

The majority of users fall within the age group of 22-24 years, suggesting that young adults are the primary customer base. Efforts to engage this demographic more effectively could yield positive results. The significant loss of users, especially among males, indicates a need for improved customer retention strategies.

Business Focus Recommendations

Enhance Customer Retention:

  • Conduct surveys and gather feedback from lost users to understand the reasons behind their departure.
  • Implement loyalty programs and personalized offers to retain existing users.
  • Improve customer support and user experience on the platform.

Target Young Adult Demographic:

  • Create marketing campaigns and promotions tailored to young adults, leveraging social media platforms popular with this age group.
  • Introduce meal options and offers that cater to the preferences of users aged 22-24.

Expand and Optimize Services in High-Performing Cities:

  • Focus on Tirupati, Electronic City, Baner, and Bikaner for further expansion and marketing efforts.
  • Invest in local partnerships and promotions to strengthen the brand presence in these areas.

Diversify Food Options:

  • Given the preference for foods with vegetables, introduce new vegetarian and healthy meal options.
  • Promote other food categories to balance revenue distribution and cater to diverse user preferences.

Analyze and Adapt to Market Trends:

  • Regularly analyze sales data and market trends to identify shifts in consumer behavior.
  • Adapt the business strategy based on these insights to stay competitive and relevant in the market.

By focusing on these areas, Zomato can enhance its user experience, improve customer retention, and drive further growth in its operations.
