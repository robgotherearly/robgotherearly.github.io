Hotel Sales Data Analysis

Introduction

This project delves into the intricacies of hotel revenue, booking trends, and guest preferences through a meticulous blend of database construction, SQL querying, and Power BI visualization.

Building the Database

The project commenced with the assembly of hotel booking data, meticulously curated within an Excel spreadsheet. Subsequently, the data was imported into SQL Server Studio, laying the groundwork for streamlined data querying and analysis.

Developing the SQL Query

Harnessing the power of SQL queries, I deftly extracted pertinent data, ensuring utmost accuracy and precision in the information retrieved for subsequent analysis.

Connecting Power BI to the Database

Seamlessly integrating Power BI with the SQL Server database facilitated a smooth transition from raw data to visually captivating representations, setting the stage for insightful analysis.

Visualizing the Data

The crux of the project involved breathing life into critical insights. Through meticulous visualization efforts, I delved into total revenue growth trends across years, dissecting them by hotel type. Standout discoveries included a remarkable revenue surge from $1.7 million in 2018 to $7.93 million in 2019, further escalating to an impressive $14.79 million in 2020. The revenue distribution between Resort and City hotels unveiled a split of 46.3% and 53.7%, respectively.

Summarizing the Findings

The analysis unearthed pivotal trends, prompting consideration for the expansion of parking facilities owing to escalating demand for car spaces. Fluctuations in the Average Daily Rate (ADR) hinted at potential seasonality in booking patterns. The overarching conclusion underscored the triumph of the Excel to Power BI data journey and the invaluable insights gleaned, underscoring the imperative of data-driven decision-making processes.

Conclusion

he journey from Excel to Power BI proved instrumental in extracting meaningful insights from hotel booking data. With a focus on revenue trends, parking needs, and key data patterns, the project showcased the power of data analysis in making informed decisions. The call to stay data-driven emphasized a commitment to ongoing success and adaptability in the dynamic hospitality industry.
