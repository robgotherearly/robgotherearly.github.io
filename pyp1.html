Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) analysis

RFM Analysis Project

RFM analysis is a technique used by businesses to segment their customers based on their past behavior. The acronym stands for Recency, Frequency, and Monetary Value. Here's a breakdown of each component:

  1. Recency: This refers to how recently a customer has made a purchase. Customers who have made a purchase more recently are often considered more valuable because they are likely still engaged with the brand or product.
  2. Frequency: Frequency measures how often a customer makes a purchase within a given period of time. Customers who make frequent purchases are usually more loyal and engaged with the brand.
  3. Monetary Value: This represents the amount of money a customer has spent over a specific period of time. Customers who spend more money are typically more valuable to the business.

RFM analysis involves assigning a score to each of these three metrics for each customer, based on their historical transaction data. For example, customers who made a purchase recently might receive a higher recency score, while those who make frequent purchases might receive a higher frequency score.

After assigning scores, businesses typically group customers into segments based on these scores. For instance, customers with high scores in all three categories might be considered "high-value" customers, while those with low scores across the board might be considered "low-value" customers.

Once segmented, businesses can tailor their marketing strategies and offerings to better meet the needs and preferences of each customer segment, ultimately improving customer satisfaction and loyalty. RFM analysis helps businesses identify their most valuable customers, predict future behavior, and optimize marketing efforts to maximize profitability.
